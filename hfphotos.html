Grow 1:
Cinderella 99 & Big Band

These two cultivars were grown indoors using "Mambo soil", an organic super soil cultivated by a local agricultural supplier in D.C. Cap City Organics . They were my first ever successful attempt at growing indoors and each yielded about 10 ounces. I had never tried either cultivar prior to growing them; however, Cindy 99 quickly rose to the top of our list as a new favorite strain.
learn more about it here.

Grow 2:
Forbidden Fruit Cake & DoSiDos

After learning a lot about nutrient deficiencies and a proper watering schedule based on the plant's actual needs, I decided to undertake another grow. This time I planted two premuim cultivars produced by The Plug titled "Forbidden Fruit Cake" & "DoSiDos". Grown in Coast of Maine Stonington Blend, this time I yielded about half a pound per plant! We couldn't believe how monstrous and dense each plant became. As you can tell by the pictures, they each grew to be between 4 and 6 ft tall! The odor was also incredible, it smelled like the sweetest fruitiest candy, and tasted just as delicious. It was pricey but well worth the effort.

Grow 3:
Blue Diesel Auto

Grown in Cap City Organics' new receipe for their Mambo soil, my first attempt at growing outside was a great success; however, I will say I sacrificed a lot in yield. I am still unsure as to why it didnt yield as much since it was long-growing auto plant. However, I suspect that maybe they were stunted from accidental supercropping. This strain also wasn't as fragrant as Forbidden Fruitcake, DoSiDos or Cindy 99, the last of which smelled like bubbalicious bubblegum! Nonetheless, it was the perfect sativa experience and I would love to try it again.
